THIRUVANATHAPARAM










Thiruvananthapuram is a major tourist centre, known for the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, the beaches of Kovalam and Varkala, the backwaters of Poovar and Anchuthengu and its Western Ghats tracts of Ponmudi and the Agastya Mala.Thiruvananthapuram, or Trivandrum, as it was conveniently re-christened by the English, is the southern-most district and Thiruvananthapuram city is the district headquarters as well as the State capital of Kerala.Geography. The district stretches along the shores of Arabian Sea for a distance of 78 km. On geographical basis, the district can be divided into three regions – highland, midland and lowland. Thiruvananthapuram, Chirayinkeezhu and Varkala taluks lie in the midland and lowland regions.
